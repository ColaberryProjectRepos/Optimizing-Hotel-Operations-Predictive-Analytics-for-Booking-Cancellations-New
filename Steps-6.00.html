Visualization: Case 1- Optimizing Booking Channels and Lead Times 6
Positive Insight: 

Analysis shows that direct bookings with a lead time of over 90 days have a 45% lower cancellation rate compared to other channels. This is beneficial as it ensures more stable revenue streams from long-term bookings. 





What-if Analysis

Forecast Cancellation rate by Adjusting Lead time (+/- 10%) of the average 

Analysis shows that a +10% adjustment in lead time results in a 89% lower cancellation for direct bookings with a lead time of over 90 days compared to other channels.





Analysis also, shows that a -10% adjustment in lead time results in only 43% lower cancellation for direct bookings with a lead time of over 90 days compared to other channels.





Benefits of what-if analysis:

The interactive nature of the Lead Time Adjustment makes it a powerful tool for analyzing and validating the relationship between lead times and cancellation rates, supporting strategic adjustments in marketing and reservation policies.

Negative Insight:

Bookings from third-party agents with short lead times (less than 30 days) exhibit a 64% increase in cancellation rates compared to direct bookings. This volatility can affect resource planning and revenue predictability.





Case 1 ROI:
  • By focusing marketing efforts on promoting direct bookings for long lead times, and potentially offering incentives, the hotel can increase direct bookings by 10%, potentially boosting annual revenue by 7% and achieving an ROI of 25%.
