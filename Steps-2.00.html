Executive Summary 2

This project showcases the application of analytics in the hospitality industry to manage and mitigate hotel cancellations effectively. Utilizing a classifier on historical reservation data, the project significantly reduced cancellations, particularly for long-term bookings and guests with special requests. Insights revealed that optimizing booking channels and understanding customer segments can lead to substantial improvements in revenue stability and guest satisfaction, underscoring the value of data-driven strategies in enhancing operational efficiency and customer experiences.


Background

In the hospitality sector, accurately forecasting cancellations is crucial for efficient resource allocation and improved guest experiences. By analyzing historical booking data, this project optimizes booking strategies and enhances operational efficiency.



Objectives

To develop a robust, data-driven model for accurately predicting hotel cancellations, enabling hotels to optimize resource allocation, improve customer satisfaction, and enhance operational efficiency through proactive decision-making.



Problem Statement

Hotel cancellations pose a significant challenge, with up to 40% of reservations being canceled globally. This project address the critical need to predict cancellations accurately, allowing hotels to optimize their resource allocation and improve customer satisfaction. By analyzing factors such as arrival date, lead time, and market segment, we aim to develop a robust model that enables proactive decision-making in the face of uncertain booking patterns.
